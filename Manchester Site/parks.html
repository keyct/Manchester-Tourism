Parks & Recreation

Manchester's portion of the acclaimed James River Parks System is a unique blend of man-made structures and the beauty of the natural fall-line environment.

One of the last remaining remnants of the Richmond and Petersburg Railroad Bridge
which spanned the James River for much of the 1800s.
Today the wall is a popular rock-climbing destination.
An overlook with views of the city skyline is present at its crest.
Climbers gather around a support structure of the long departed railroad bridge.
The impressive stones are made from granite quarried directly from the river itself.
The T. Tyler Potterfield Memorial Bridge, a pedestrian bridge completed in 2016,
is constructed on a former dam that is well over a century old.
